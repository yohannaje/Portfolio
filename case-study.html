The place where just a few breathes can change your life

The Mission

Breathology was founded with a single purpose, improve peoples lives through their most basic habit, breathing. This way, Breathology offers coaches and guided sessions so everyone at home, at the office or any other place, can take a few minutes to improve their breathing.

Inhale, Exhale

We took meditation, breathing and relaxation habits into consideration for the creation of the logo, one of the habits that most resonated with the founders, was stacking stones to achieve a status of mindfulness.

From here, we took the organic shapes of these stones, and combined them with the movement of breathing, inhaling, and exhaling, and that path our breath creates. From here, we designed the logo.

The Logo

After deconstructing the stone balancing elements, we added the design grid and we placed proportional circles to mantain consistency accross the final piece.

Typography

Nunito is Breathology’s primary font for web, print, promo materials, and social media events. When people see this font they should think Breathology. With that said, we’ve paired the font Work Sans to be used soley as a body copy or other necessary alternations.

Brand Colors

Breathology has five primary colors that contribute to the brand. Deep Night, Pelican Bay, and Alabaster can be used for larger color fills and also using the Pelican Bay and Silver to be used for color accents or to highlights. Royal blue is used as another solid or accent color.

Pelican Bay was chosen for the product because substantial research supports that shades of blue calls to mind feelings of calmness and serenity, evoking peace, safety and tranquility.

The App

We kept the app minimal and clean, during our workshops we identified its personas, and user journeys (our next case study!) and we followed along with shapes that were organic and matched the brand aesthetic.

We incorporated geometrical elements with a thin stroke to provide better accents and focus over the solid shapes that represented backgrounds.

Going Digital

Something that our workshops showed is that most persona users didnt want to feel let down. They needed to be able to prove to their family, friends and clients that these suggestions were backed up with real data or scientific research.

This is why in our designs for communications and complimentary content, we encouraged the brand to teach and show their data and deliver it from the professionals themselves even to make their users feel safe.
